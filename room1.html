HISTORICAL Muslim Tourism

Welcome to Room 1, let's talk about the history of tourism among muslims.

Now that we all know about halal tourism, Let's get deeper! In this room, we will understand how it all started.

Muslim men of literature throughout the middle centuries had an obsession with travel. This is the reason they were so passionate about perfecting the art of travel. The journey served as their biggest mission yet, for the majority of them. Muslims viewed travel as a symbol of metamorphosis combined with a challenging experience because it lead them into learning as a way of life via suffering. They probably wouldn't have understood why some people viewed travel as a form of "servitude," a "burden" on fulfilling work that resulted in a "incidental loss of weeks or months" and made them experience "periods of hunger, exhaustion, sickness perhaps; and always the thousand and one dreary tasks which eat away the days to no purpose."

Certain types of travelling have always been encouraged by Islamic doctrine throughout the history. One is the express requirement to make the hajj (pilgrimage to Mecca). Another is hijra, which is the duty to leave places where practicing Islam is restricted in order to go to places where there are, in theory, no such restrictions. Ziyaras (going to local or regional shrines) and rihla (traveling to seek knowledge) are two other instances of travel that is motivated by religion.

However, other modes of transportation that are not included in theology may be just as important or even more so. In the beginning of Islam, muslims frequently combined business travel with religious travel. Travel and rihla were coupled by Moroccan travelers in the fifteenth through seventeenth centuries, as well as Dyula travelers in the nineteenth and twentieth centuries. Just as the hijra, the migration of the Prophet and his followers from Mecca to Medina in 622, became a dominant theme in Islamic political though, Walzer and therefore travelling has always be seen as an important feature of Islam.

Pilgrimage tourism
One of the earliest and most fundamental types of population tourism known to human society is pilgrimage, which has had and continues to have significant political, social, cultural, and economic ramifications. Islam has not been an exception and therefore from the very beginning of Islam, thousands of muslims travelled to the holly city of mecca to fulfill the act of their religious verdics.

Muslim Arab Merchants
You might be thinking Pilgrimage was the first attempt of muslims for travelling but that is not true. Arab merchants have a long history going back to many centuries ago. They were essential to the growth of trade routes and the movement of goods between various geographical areas. Arab merchants were active in trade and had a considerable impact on regional trade even before the advent of Islam. They created networks of commerce throughout the Arabian Peninsula and were renowned for their prowess in the caravan trade and this is a sketch of that era.
