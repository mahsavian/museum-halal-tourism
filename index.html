Welcome to the Digital Exhibit

Halal tourism is "a subset of tourism activities that comply with Islamic rules and principles, allowing Muslim travelers to enjoy tourism activities without violating their religious doctrines," according to a study by Moghavvemi and Ismail. Halal tourism refers to travel activities that are created to meet the requirements of Muslim tourists who desire to uphold Islamic principles while on the road. This covers having access to places for praying, halal food, and other services that are suitable for their religion. In recent years, as Muslim tourists have increased and the travel industry has adapted to meet their unique needs, the idea of halal tourism has drawn more and more attention.
