Regulations of Halal Tourism

Welcome to Room 3, let's talk about the specifications of Halal tourism.

Halal tourism is the type of tourism that adheres to the values of Islam, bearing in mind that religion influences the day-to-day activities of Muslims—whether at home or traveling—and thus shapes the choice of a destination for discretionary purposes and what is done at the destination (Jafari and Scott, 2014).

It refers to “any tourism object or action which is permissible according to Islamic teachings for Muslims to use or engage in as part of the tourism industry” (Battour and Ismail, 2016, p. 151).

Halal tourism can be defined as the provision of tour packages and locations that are created to satisfy the demands of Muslim travelers in a way that is consistent with their religious duties. As a result, it comprises elements like travel, lodging, meals, and any other tourist services that follow the doctrine of Islam and provide a fun experience while following Islamic regulations.

It is assumed that vacation destinations are associated with joy and contentment. In this model, halal tourism is marketed to tourists seeking entertainment, while allowing them to deepen their faith by appreciating God's beauty. As a result, visitors can stay away from polytheism, immorality, waste, exaggeration, and ill deeds (Ariffin, 2020).

The five basic components of Leiper's Tourism System (1979) model: the tourist, the tourist generating region, the tourist destination region, the tourist industry, and the transit route.

These elements cover destinations, accommodations, foods, activities, religious facilities, and even the social environment. Since many of our visitors are non-Muslims and may not have a clear picture of the Islamic regulations for each of these elements, we have simplified the most important 9 regulations below. The 9 Key Regulations

1. Dog-Free Spaces

As dogs are not typically accepted for pleasure within the doctrine of Islam, it is necessary for Islamic tour providers to ensure dog-free spaces for Muslim tourists. This means avoiding entertainment or matches involving dogs.

2. Alcohol Prohibitions

According to Islam, it is strictly prohibited to drink alcohol. It is essential for the industry to provide environments that do not offer drinks or host drinking parties.

3. Entertainment Restrictions

Music and dance are generally discouraged in Islamic practice. Hotels and tour providers should consider this when planning evening activities to ensure they are appropriate for this context.

4. Modesty and Clothing

Regulations regarding clothing are a crucial discourse in Islam. Hotel staff, tour operators, and the destination environment should be adapted to these norms of modesty.

5. Gender Separation (Mahram)

In Islam, men and women who are not married or do not have close blood connections (Namahram) should not be in confined, close spaces together. Tours must be careful in planning group activities and accommodation.

6. Sanitation Facilities

Muslims require specific access to water for ritual purification (Wudu) before prayers. Organizers and accommodations must provide appropriate facilities for this.

7. Religious Guidelines

8. Prayer Facilities

Prayer times must be considered in itinerary planning. Hotels should provide a quiet prayer room and facilities for ritual washing (Wudu).

9. Halal Food Issues

Halal food is critical. Tours must ensure there is no haram meat, no alcohol, and no pork/pork gelatine. Trust in the organizer is paramount for Muslim travelers when verifying these ingredients.
